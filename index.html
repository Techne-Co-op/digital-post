Concept

The Digital Post

A civic institution for reliable communication, operated locally as cooperatives, connected globally through protocol, grounded in the human right to seek, receive, and impart information.

Precedent

The postal service is not a delivery company. It is one of the oldest civic institutions in modern democracy. Before telephones, before telegraph in most places, the post was the infrastructure that made self-governance possible at distance. The Universal Postal Union, founded in 1874, solved a coordination problem that looked impossible: how do sovereign nations, with different currencies and different interests, agree to carry each other's mail?

The answer was not a shared currency or a central authority. It was a protocol. Published rate cards, bilateral clearing, and a constitutional commitment to universal service.

No equivalent institution exists for the digital world. Cloud providers are not common carriers. Social platforms are not public utilities. ISPs are not post offices. The institution described here fills that vacancy.

Foundation
Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 19
Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression; this right includes freedom to hold opinions without interference and to seek, receive and impart information and ideas through any media and regardless of frontiers.

The postal service was the institutional expression of this right in the physical world. The Digital Post is its expression in the digital world. Not dependent on any single state, but legible to all of them. Not adversarial to jurisdiction, but independent of it.

Translation

The postal service runs on physical infrastructure: sorting facilities, trucks, mailboxes, post offices. The Digital Post runs on four equivalent primitives.

Postal Infrastructure Digital Primitive
Sorting and processing Compute. The work of transforming, routing, and responding.
Trucks, routes, carriers Transfer. Moving data between points.
PO Boxes, holding counters Short-term memory. Fast-access state for active use.
Archives, dead letter office Long-term memory. Durable storage across time.

The stamp is the unit of prepaid access. You buy it before you need the service. Its value is tied to the work it buys, not to speculation. When you are ready to send, you use it. The Digital Post generalizes this into a service credit: a prepaid claim on a defined quantity of compute, transfer, or memory, issued by a cooperative, redeemable whenever you are ready.

Stable value comes from stable utility.

Architecture

The critical design move: the function the postal service performs (reliable, universal, affordable communication) does not require the form of a state agency. A cooperative of cooperatives, each operating local infrastructure, connected through a shared protocol, governed by a constitution grounded in human rights, can perform the same function.

Constitution
Human Rights Charter
Grounded in UDHR Article 19. Universal service obligation. Data sovereignty. Democratic governance. Not a terms of service, a constitutional commitment.
Protocol
The Clearing Interface
Published rate cards per primitive. Bilateral clearing between nodes. No shared token, no central authority. The UPU model, digitized. Any conforming node can stand alone; the value scales with the network.
Attestation
Proof of Capacity
Monthly attestations: outstanding credit supply, reserve ratios, operational capacity. Published to an open ledger. The guarantee is backed by capacity to deliver, not by held assets.
Nodes
Local Cooperatives
Each node is a member-governed cooperative operating physical infrastructure in its community. Servers, network connections, storage. Locally owned, locally maintained. The building committed for 100 years.
Credit
The Stamp
Prepaid service credits issued by each node. Redeemable for compute, transfer, and memory at published rates. Transferable between members. Not a security, not a currency. A promise that work will be done.
Applications

Every application a city, a cooperative, or a community needs to run is a composition of the same four primitives in varying proportions. The Ethereum Foundation's Verifiable Cities program identifies six. Each one runs on this infrastructure.

01
Programmable Policy
Policy logic as structured, testable rules. Compute for execution, memory for state, transfer for notification.
02
Outcomes-Based Contracts
Milestone verification and payment routing. The attestation layer provides the audit trail.
03
Privacy-Preserving IDs
Selective disclosure credentials. Compute for zero-knowledge proofs, memory for credential state.
04
Cross-Agency Verification
Confirm facts across boundaries without transferring data. The clearing interface, applied to governance.
05
Verifiable Credentials
Permits and licenses with live revocation. Long-term memory for the registry, compute for verification.
06
Public Works Visibility
The invisible labor of maintenance, made legible. Every action timestamped, auditable, defensible.

A municipality that joins the network as a node, or partners with a local cooperative node, gets access to all six capabilities as composed services, priced in credits, auditable through the attestation layer, governed by a constitution rather than a vendor contract.

Orientation

There is an information right alongside the housing right. The first century of computing was shaped by corporate co-option of tools originally conceived for collective coordination. The Digital Post recovers the older pattern: infrastructure operated by the community it serves, connected to a global network through protocol rather than platform, grounded in constitutional commitments that outlast any single operator.

The first node exists. The protocol is specified. The applications are identified. What remains is the network.
